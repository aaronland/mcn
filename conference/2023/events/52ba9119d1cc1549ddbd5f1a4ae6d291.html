Making High-tech Magic with Low-Tech Materials

Log in to save this to your schedule, view media, leave feedback and see who's attending!

Tweet Share
This session will provide an overview of the process and challenges in creating the “Art Tech Tree”, a space for making physical artworks and bringing them to life through gesture-driven interactions and projections. This project is part of the Metropolitan Museum of Art’s new immersive children’s space—The 81st Street Studio. The Art Tech Tree helps kids explore printmaking and drumming, and uses computer vision and projection mapping to bring to life the physical prints and drums made by kids. 
The presentation will begin with an overview of the experience including the technology behind the magic, and the user flow between physical making and digital playing. We’ll share insights into the iterative prototyping, design, and production process. We'll conclude with a focus on our iterative user testing process, including the necessary pivots that leveraged experience design to mitigate technical limitations.
