My Oh My! How My Aquarium and MySmithsonian will Engage Audiences in an Oversaturated Digital Landscape through Direct Engagement
Tuesday October 21, 2025 10:15am - 11:00am CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
In an increasingly fragmented digital landscape where "zero-click" searches and an overwhelming number of digital channels often dilute user engagement, institutions face the challenge of creating meaningful, lasting connections with their audiences. This session will explore how the Smithsonian Institution and the Monterey Bay Aquarium plan to build resilience into their audience engagement strategies through new digital products that provide self-service tools, localization, and personalization to enhance the user experience and forge direct relationships with audiences.

The Smithsonian's "MySmithsonian" and its underlying "Online Audience Portal" (OAP), along with the Monterey Bay Aquarium's "My Aquarium" and "Project RIPPLE" initiatives, represent a strategic shift towards creating cohesive, user-centric digital ecosystems. Both institutions are leveraging Salesforce CRM to build powerful self-service platforms that empower users to manage their tickets, donations, and memberships online.

Building on this transactional foundation, MySmithsonian and My Aquarium will offer personalized content and localized experiences. By sharing a unified authentication system with their respective portals, these platforms will provide a seamless user journey. We will delve into the critical questions surrounding this approach: How do we balance the potential friction of user registration with the long-term payoff of a personalized relationship? How can we attract and retain digital visitors who may never visit in person, and how do we maintain a connection long after a physical visit? How might this technology empower our organizations to provide long-lasting learning and inspiration for our users?

This session will offer a transparent look at the opportunities and challenges of each project’s tech stack, the strategy behind leveraging first-party data to personalize content delivery, and the value of strengthening digital relationships in order to support institutional resilience. We will discuss how initial user research shaped these projects and the metrics we will use to measure success in this new paradigm of digital transformation, moving from passive consumption to active, personal engagement.
