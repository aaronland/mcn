Where is the #musetech community online?
Tuesday October 21, 2025 3:15pm - 4:00pm CDT
Barnes Building, 8th Floor - Garden Terrace Room
You're checked in!
Where did the #musetech community and institutions go after Twitter became X? We propose holding a campfire session, where presenters spend a few minutes discussing why they are holding the session, then opening up the remaining time for audience discussion/participation. We’ll discuss the background of #musetech on Twitter, and how some audiences are known to be in specific places now (Digital Humanities community is largely on Bluesky). Then attendees will be invited to put dots up on easel pads for what platforms they are on and whether they are Visitor or Resident according to David O. White Visitor/Resident model of online behavior, as well as if they do social media for an organization or their personal accounts. We will have a discussion about everyone’s thoughts on whether we should try to recreate the online community that we had in the 2010’s on Twitter, and where are institutions going in the post X era? Where do institutions collaborate? Larger metropolitan areas tend to have professional networks where social media managers can collaborate, do managers from less populated areas have similar networks? If not, where do they go? While hashtag campaigns used to go well on Twitter, what’s working now?

This session is part 2 of a 3 step endeavor: Step one: We plan to send a survey out to social media managers and other #musetech professionals to get a wide variety of organizations and professionals involved. Step two: hold the campfire session. Step three: publish results of the survey and session in a venue that will be useful to the #musetech community.
