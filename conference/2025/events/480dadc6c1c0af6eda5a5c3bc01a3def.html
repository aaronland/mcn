A case study in making a multimodal map-based data visualization
Wednesday October 22, 2025 2:15pm - 2:35pm CDT
Barnes Building, 8th Floor - Garden Terrace Room
You're checked in!
For the new History of Lynching in Maryland exhibit at the Reginald F. Lewis Museum in Baltimore, Quatrefoil Associates was tasked with creating an interactive media experience featuring soil samples from lynching sites around Maryland. We wanted to give visitors a personal experience while being respectful of these artifacts. We also needed to inform visitors about the victims and leave them with an understanding the lynching was widespread throughout Maryland. We created a projected video containing a map of Maryland which can show up to three pop-ups with information about individual victims. These are triggered by capacitive labels in front of each vessel. We consulted with Dr. Cheryl Fogle-Hatch on how to make this experience accessible to blind and low-vision visitors. Because it would be difficult to create a full interface for this interactive that doesn’t require vision, we decided to add an alternate format instead. A tactile map placed next to the vessels shows the same map with points at each of the lynching sites, braille labels, and a QR code to access the full text.

This presentation, by the interactive producer responsible for creating the digital elements and their multiple interfaces for the exhibit, will present their strategy and learnings from the project. The presenter welcomes discussion from session participants about universal design approaches to creating multiple interfaces to museum experiences, the strengths, drawbacks, and pitfalls to avoid.
