One Product, Many Perspectives: A Case Study in Collaborative Design for Digital Interpretation
Tuesday October 21, 2025 3:15pm - 3:35pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!


What happens when a UX researcher, an interpretation specialist, and a product lead come together to build a digital storytelling kiosk? How do their different perspectives, roles, and motivations drive the project forward, especially with tight deadlines?

This case study from the National Gallery shares how a cross-functional team developed an interactive platform to guide visitors through close-looking experiences with small, detailed artworks. Designed as both an MVP and a reusable interpretive tool, the kiosk combines storytelling and high-res image zoom powered by IIIF and DAM integration.

Each presenter offers a different lens—on user needs, curatorial goals, and technical implementation—highlighting where their perspectives clashed and connected. The talk emphasizes the need for continuous dispersal of assumptions between team members, how they managed shifting expectations, scoped content, and kept collaboration open and visitor-centered.

 Ideal for small teams, this session offers practical takeaways on working across disciplines, launching imperfect products, and iterating based on real user needs.
