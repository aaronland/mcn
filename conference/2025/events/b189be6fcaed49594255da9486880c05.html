From Exhibits to Platforms: software infrastructure at the Exploratorium
Wednesday October 22, 2025 2:40pm - 3:00pm CDT
Barnes Building, 8th Floor - Garden Terrace Room
You're checked in!
Software-based exhibits in science museums are often developed as one-offs.. Developers are often tempted, therefore, to start coding every exhibit from scratch. Some features, however, are common to many types of exhibits, especially screen-centered ones: support for multilingual text, communication with microcontrollers, and persistent memory are only some examples.

At the Exploratorium, we created a template that includes these capabilities out of the box, allowing developers to focus on exhibit-specific features, but also contributing to a shared coding style that makes exhibits easier to maintain across our New Media team. This template gave rise to other infrastructure projects, such as WOMBAT, a local authoring tool allowing non-technical collaborators, such as science writers and evaluators, to edit text and other parameters at the exhibit. This, in practice, turns the exhibit into a platform, where not just minor tweeks can be edited by non-technical collaborators, but the entire physical and digital build of the exhibit can serve completely different types of content.

In this session, we will showcase the capabilities of the template and WOMBAT through two examples. First, we will explore Seeing Scientifically, an exhibit that allows visitors to explore live samples at a microscope with the assistance of AI, and how WOMBAT allows partner institutions to use their own organisms and write their own content. Then, we will explore How Much Grandma, a digitally-enhanced scroll mixing linear storytelling with visitor interactivity that tells the story of genetic inheritance. We will emphasize how WOMBAT was used for agile content development and evaluation, while enhancing collaboration across the project team.
