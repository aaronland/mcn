Building the Social Forest: An Interactive Curriculum Rooted in Tongva Knowledge
Tuesday October 21, 2025 11:40am - 12:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
Social Forest: Oaks of Tovaangar is a digital curriculum designed by The Broad in partnership with Urban Insight and Tongva authors. Part of the Getty PST Art: Art + Science Collide initiative, the project connects students with the art and social impact of Broad collection artist Joseph Beuys while centering Indigenous knowledge systems into a new educational experience for middle and high school students, designed to be exploratory, interactive, and culturally grounded.

This session shares lessons from a collaboration between museum educators and a technical development team. From The Broad’s perspective, the goal was to work with Native contributors to create an interactive curriculum that emphasizes the interconnectedness of people, land, science, and art from a distinctly Indigenous perspective. The result is a classroom-ready tool that blends audio, imagery, and discussion prompts in a structure that resists linear storytelling, inviting students to explore at their own pace.

From the technical side, the solution uses a progressively decoupled architecture with a shared Drupal backend and a custom Next.js frontend. Urban Insight developed a replicable system for managing content using layout paragraphs, allowing content editors to define grid layouts without developer involvement. A custom Drupal module transforms structured data into a frontend-ready JSON schema, powering an interactive experience optimized for Chromebooks and a touchscreen kiosk mode in-gallery. We will highlight key learnings and technical challenges from the implementation, including how we balanced performance, editorial flexibility, and the unique needs of an interactive educational interface.

Together, the presenters will explore how the project was conceptualized and built—from authoring educational content with cultural sensitivity, to creating a modular and flexible content model, to designing for nontraditional navigation and touchscreen use. The session highlights how thoughtful collaboration can produce an experience that is both technically sound and pedagogically meaningful.
