Moving Through the Layers: Creating an Immersive Conservation Experience
Tuesday October 21, 2025 3:40pm - 4:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
The conservation of Pintoricchio’s Virgin and Child offered a rare opportunity to visualize a complex conservation process through digital storytelling. How could we create an experience where visitors move through each layer of conservation, almost as the conservator had? This question led to Pintoricchio Magnified, a first-of-its-kind immersive conservation experience.

Hidden beneath layers of an early 20th-century restoration, the Virgin and Child painting was revived through meticulous treatment in the Cleveland Museum of Art’s Painting Conservation Lab. To support visitor understanding of the process, we developed an immersive digital experience in a traditional gallery space of our permanent collection, where technology is rarely utilized.

Led by the Digital Innovations team in collaboration with the Painting Conservation team, the experience invites guests to explore the conservation story interactively. As visitors move through the space, a 10-by-10-foot digital display responds to their movements, allowing them to amplify and study specific areas of the painting, as if viewing it through a microscope and toggle between technical and treatment imaging layers, revealing the process step by step.

Rather than relying on touchscreens or fast-paced interaction, the experience encourages slow looking. Visitors engage at their own pace, with the system adjusting subtly to their position. This meditative approach supports close observation and a deeper appreciation for the painting’s materials and structure. The system uses computer vision to track movement and adjust content in real time. Its flexible design suggests future applications for responsive experiences across gallery spaces.

This session will explore the full lifecycle of Pintoricchio Magnified: An Immersive Conservation Experience, from concept to design to implementation, sharing insights on:

- Translating technical material into accessible, public-facing interpretive experiences

- Methods for inclusive engagement across a wide range of audiences

- Expanding collaboration from focused expert groups to broader internal and external partnerships

- Integrating interactive technology within the constraints of traditional spaces

- Surfacing behind-the-scenes content in responsible and engaging ways

- Strategies for demystifying complex topics and highlighting the often-unseen labor of museum professionals

- Using analytics and evaluation to gain insights and inform future projects

This session invites museum professionals, technologists, and storytellers to explore how immersive digital interpretation can deepen public engagement in behind-the-scenes work and support meaningful innovation.
