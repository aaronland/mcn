Freedom Reimagined: Interactive Storytelling and Cultural Legacy in the Transformation of Miami’s Historic Freedom Tower
Tuesday October 21, 2025 2:40pm - 3:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
In September 2025, Miami Dade College reopened the iconic Freedom Tower following a $30 million, multi-year restoration. Originally built in 1925 as the Miami News headquarters and later known as “El Refugio” for its role supporting Cuban refugees, the National Historic Landmark now enters a new chapter as a civic and cultural institution defined by storytelling, technology, and inclusive design.

MDC engaged Local Projects to envision an inclusive, interactive experience that would honor the past while engaging a very diverse audience. This presentation will explore how the Freedom Tower’s new exhibitions use immersive media, responsive environments, and participatory storytelling to activate memory and identity. These exhibitions invite reflection on migration, resilience, and belonging through a blend of historic artifacts, digital archives, oral histories, and contemporary art.

The session will also examine the challenges and strategies behind integrating state-of-the-art digital hardware into a restored Mediterranean Revival structure without compromising its historic integrity. 

The Freedom Tower’s transformation offers a compelling case study in using technology not for spectacle, but as a tool for empathy, education, and enduring cultural relevance.
