Land Back, Data Back, Everything Back: Respecting Tribal Sovereignty in Digital Project Design
Tuesday October 21, 2025 10:15am - 11:00am CDT
Bazinet Lobby - Cinema
You're checked in!
This session will explore the process  co-developed by First Americans Museum and The Experience Alchemists to design an online educator resource that will provide audiences across the U.S. with high-quality, Native-produced social studies resources. The presenters will walk through the process of conducting requirements reviews with both tribal stakeholders and potential audiences, developing an information architecture that is responsive to indigenous data sovereignty needs, and the process to review and gather assets, as well as integrating Native-developed materials in existing digital metadata schema.

The topics will be:

- Co-creating digital resources with Native communities, aka “how (hopefully) not to colonize the digital realm”

- Tribal data sovereignty

- Deciding when and how to relate to state and federal ed standards.

- Designing design processes with non-designers

The event will consist of:

- Ice Breaker

- The FAM Online Educator Resource (OER) process thus far:

-- What it is, what it’s attempting to do

-- Why it matters,

-- How it’s different,

-- What process we developed to maximize the relationship building necessary for this tool to succeed.

- Learnings Thus Far, including:

-- Relationships, relationships, relationships. It’s all about building relationships 

-- Being culturally responsive means more than you think it does.

-- Once you start to question any part of the digital edifice, you have to question all the parts and decide how far you’re going to stray from the culturally White and Western norms of the Web.

- Q & A
