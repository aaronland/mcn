Digital Signage Gets a Makeover: Integrated, Intuitive, Immersive
Wednesday October 22, 2025 1:40pm - 1:55pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
Digital signage is nothing new in museums. The real challenge is making it effective by delivering clear, timely information that enhances the visitor experience without putting extra strain on a small team.  

This session shares how to create a signage system that goes beyond static schedules. The system adapts in real time to changes in exhibitions, tours, performances, and digital programs. It gives visitors the ability to act, such as buying tickets directly from their phones. It stays visually cohesive with the historic Breuer architecture and CMA brand. Most importantly, it’s built to be sustainable, easy to update, manage, and scale with limited staff and resources.  

During the recent renovation and restoration of the CMA’s historic Breuer lobby, we had a unique opportunity to rethink the role of digital signage. The project called for a large-scale screen that would act as a central information hub; distinct from the interactive ArtLens Wall but held to the same high standards for design, clarity, and experience.  

We developed a streamlined, visually elegant signage system using our existing digital infrastructure. Content updates automatically in real time, pulling from systems already in use across the museum. Each event or program is paired with a QR code, allowing visitors to learn more or buy tickets instantly from their phones. The layout is intuitive, highlighting what is happening now and next, and features artwork from the collection to inspire deeper exploration throughout the galleries.  

This session will cover the strategies we used to create signage aligned with institutional digital goals, focusing on key priorities:  

1. One source of truth: Updates made once are automatically reflected everywhere (on signage, the website, and anywhere someone interacts with our digital products).  

2. Fully integrated backend: Seamless system integration within our existing digital infrastructure ensures signage remains up and accurate. 

3. Low content management lift: Designed for a busy team with minimal need for manual updates. 

4. Development team collaboration: Design and development collaboration that allows our team to truly own and manage our systems after a vendor completes their scope.    

5. Paperless ticketing: QR code access eliminates the need for kiosks.  

6. Increased visibility of our collection: Leveraging our robust collection digitization initiative to highlight 3D models, collection searches, and object locations to create more entry points into the collection, encouraging independent exploration.   

7. Modular design: A flexible structure that can evolve with the institution’s needs.

We'll share how this signage project embodies our mission to create experiences “for the benefit of all the people, forever,” by increasing awareness of everything the museum offers and shaping strong, positive first impressions. This session offers a practical roadmap for creating digital signage that works with and for your institution and your visitors.
