Future-Ready Collaborations: Digital Innovation in Cultural Spaces
Tuesday October 21, 2025 10:15am - 10:35am CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
App DigiFab, an interdisciplinary learning community at Appalachian State University, explores how collaboration empowers cultural institutions to create adaptive digital experiences. This 15-minute case study shares actionable lessons on resilience and relevance in exhibition practice.

Cultural spaces—from museums to galleries—thrive through collaboration and experimentation, crafting narratives that reflect transformation. App DigiFab evolves through exhibitions and technological shifts, refining its model by merging technology, creative disciplines, and public engagement. By partnering with educators, organizers, and institutions, it transforms cultural spaces into interactive environments that spark dialogue.

The Transformations exhibition (2022–2023) at the Turchin Center for the Visual Arts showcased this approach, integrating digital design, fabrication, and storytelling to enhance engagement. Overcoming logistical challenges—such as balancing creative deadlines—strengthened App DigiFab’s adaptability. By distributing ownership, the group sustained momentum across roles like web design, signage, copy editing, and space planning.

Expanding on these foundations, App DigiFab now leads Displacement and Resilience, an initiative exploring identity, loss, and renewal—critical themes following Hurricane Helene’s impact on Boone and the North Carolina mountains. Through structured progress reviews and shared goals, the group adapts its model to digital fabrication projects addressing displacement. Technologies such as CAD, 3D printing, CNC, AI, and interactive media enable immersive storytelling, integrating archival imagery, cultural artifacts, and community-generated content.

Public engagement remains central, with interactive talks and workshops fostering connection and collective memory amid disruption. App DigiFab seeks collaborators—including Construct3D (Brooklyn, NY) and Manifest Gallery (Cincinnati, OH)—to explore shifting relationships between people, objects, and environments. Inspired by Hurricane Helene, Displacement – This Place Meant unites artists blending traditional craft and digital techniques to reinterpret artifacts and shared spaces. Through tactile engagement and interactive storytelling, the exhibition reflects on identity, resilience, and material culture.

This session illustrates how interdisciplinary collaboration empowers cultural institutions to create responsive digital experiences that engage with complex social issues. By applying insights from Transformations and expanding its explorations of displacement, App DigiFab continues to refine collaborative exhibition practices for a dynamic and resilient future.
