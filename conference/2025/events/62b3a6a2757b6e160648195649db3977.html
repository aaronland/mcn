Can We Rely On Ai To Generate Long Description for Disabled Visitors
Tuesday October 21, 2025 2:40pm - 3:00pm CDT
Barnes Building, 8th Floor - Garden Terrace Room
You're checked in!
After conducting user research with the National Gallery of Art to understand if AI can create meaningful long descriptions that provide an equivalent experience for blind and visually impaired website visitors, we would like to share what we learned, what the reocmmendaitons form the BVI community werre, and how AI can be integrated itno our accessibility initiatives. James, a legally blind accessibility professional, conducted the research interviews with 16 blind and low vision individuals and analyzed the results to ensure the entire research project was focused on he true experience. From this research, we learned how to create prompts that help to visualize the artwork, what aspects are most important to include in the description, when to create unique prompts instead of using the same prompt every time, and how involved people still need to be in the process without overrelying on AI. It is time to embrace AI to enhance accessibility, but we must do it in a meaningful, ethical, and transparent manner.
