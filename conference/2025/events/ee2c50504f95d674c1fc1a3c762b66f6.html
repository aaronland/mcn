How Good Is Good Enough? — Taming Our AI Expectations
Wednesday October 22, 2025 11:15am - 12:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
Generative artificial intelligence is a powerful resource in helping get things done—that is, when we view it as an efficient helper and not as an autonomous thinker. But when do our expectations of AI exceed what we expect of our human colleagues? Should we hold AI to the same standards of "correct" as we do of humans? What is the minimum threshold of "correct" for us to accept AI-generated outputs?

In this session, three experts will investigate the tolerances afforded to generative AI outputs. Drawing from experiences of using AI chatbots and other AI-powered tools in their everyday duties, the speakers will describe how they each came to harnessing the benefits presented by generative AI.

From research to prototyping to fuller development and execution, the panelists will explore how they have approached AI chatbots in a similar manner to working with junior human colleagues: training them with basic instruction; refining their outputs with prompts; and ultimately putting them in a position to produce work that can continue to be refined by human expertise. Additionally, panelists will share how their hands-on experience became instrumental in shaping broader institutional policies and guidelines for AI use within their organizations.
