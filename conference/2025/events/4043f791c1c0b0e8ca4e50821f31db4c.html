Simpler, Lighter, More Effective: Rethinking Infrastructures for Online Collections
Tuesday October 21, 2025 3:15pm - 4:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
New ideas and approaches are emerging that can help museums build robust and performant online collections. 

Despite years of blood, sweat and tears, many of the old challenges remain, and in some cases, new complexity has crept in. Extracting information and media from legacy systems, building data pipelines and image services, and weaving backend systems into an engaging web platform—these are all opportunities for new thinking and innovation.

Moreover, building sustainably requires more than technology systems, it means building internal workflows, staff expertise and governance—it means building a team. There’s nothing quite like a new website and online collection to bring a museum team together.

Fresh from the launch of a new online collection, the technical group behind the new Menil.org—including internal staff, agency partners and a museum technology consultant—will lead a discussion detailing common approaches, challenges and new opportunities for building an online collection in 2025.

Introduction: What good is an online collection? MCN has a long history as a forum for discussing online collections; let’s keep pushing the state of the art forward.

Collection data: Extracting data from collections management systems and delivering it to the web is a foundational process for online collections, and it can still be a challenge. We’ll survey different approaches for data extraction; we’ll touch on the difficulties posed by legacy collections management systems and their web services; and we’ll survey the usefulness of APIs vs aggregated data files. With this context in mind, we’ll detail the Menil’s approach, which replaced a baroque aggregation of eMuseum APIs with an elegant database export to json.

Collection images: Delivering collection images to the web comes with its own challenges. We’ll discuss legacy DAMS and new cloud-based services; open-access initiatives; and the opportunities of images-as-data. At the Menil Collection, the team reconfigured an existing image pipeline with cloud-based storage and Serverless IIIF.

Web application integration: We’ll discuss different approaches, including Incremental harvesting vs bulk ingest; import into the web CMS vs lightweight data integration; how to keep in sync; performance and CDNs. For the Menil, all collection data was delivered in a single json file, updated nightly; the web application converted the json to SQLite for use in the online collection.

Values and approaches: Every architecture shows the values of the teams that built it. (Conway’s Law, etc.) We’ll talk about different infrastructure choices, and the teams behind them. At the Menil, the team prized simplicity, minimalism, and modularity, along with a clear separation of concerns.

Online collections work is an example of innovating with infrastructure: connecting core systems in new ways that promote resilience—sustainability and efficiency, and provide for resonance—engaging and thoughtful digital experiences.
