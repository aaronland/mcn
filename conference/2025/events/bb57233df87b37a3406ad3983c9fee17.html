Stories that stick: Building human connection through staff and volunteer spotlights
Wednesday October 22, 2025 11:40am - 12:00pm CDT
Barnes Building, 8th Floor - Garden Terrace Room
You're checked in!
The Monterey Bay Aquarium is well-known for sea otters, jellies, and the incredible Kelp Forest exhibit. However, it’s the people behind the scenes who truly make the Aquarium a world-class institution. 

This session will present a case study of Monterey Bay Aquarium’s “Day in the Life” social video series, which focuses on Aquarium staff and volunteers, their passion for their work, and how they contribute to our mission to inspire conservation of the ocean. 

As part of our strategic commitments for 2024-2029 to build a thriving, people-centered, and fully bilingual organization, we’ll explore how we’re telling more inclusive stories, navigating translation and AI, and keeping our voice authentic in a fast-changing digital world. We’ll also spotlight the often-unseen roles—from AV techs to volunteer guides—that run the Aquarium every day.

These videos help create authentic human connections for our internal and external audiences and reinforce organizational culture for our staff. They offer our digital audiences inspiring, relatable examples of what ocean conservation work can look like. They support the recruitment efforts of our People & Culture division by showing that the Aquarium is a place where people from all walks of life can belong, contribute, and thrive. Ultimately, these videos remind us that museums and cultural spaces have the power to inspire not just through exhibits, but through the people who bring them to life.

In this session, we’ll highlight how storytelling not only reflects a shared purpose but also invites audiences to connect the message to their own personal aspirations, experiences, and sense of meaning. We’ll also share general insights that demonstrate the power of visual storytelling to connect people with purpose.
