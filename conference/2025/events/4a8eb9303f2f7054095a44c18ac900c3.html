Empowering Institutional Priorities and Strategic Planning with Data
Tuesday October 21, 2025 3:15pm - 4:00pm CDT
Bazinet Lobby - Cinema
You're checked in!
A good strategy helps a complex organization stay mission-focused and serves as a roadmap for guiding decision-making and resource allocation. Developing measures and targets is an essential part of the strategic process, but putting them into practice can be challenging without the fundamental data and processes in place to support them. Gone wrong, this can result in locking in on the wrong things. When done well, it can fuel an iterative and virtuous cycle of critical thinking, which can inform prioritization and help build data culture.

This session, co-presented by the National Gallery of Art’s Chief Strategy and Planning Officer and its data analytics and audience development teams, will share the National Gallery's approach to using cross-functional data to illustrate its impact and support strategic metrics at all levels of the organization. We will walk through the evolution of our strategic plan and the iterative process of determining measures and developing dashboards to track these metrics in ways that promote transparency and accountability. Participants will hear tangible examples of how this applies in practice to key areas of any museum, including visitation, public and educational programming, and interpretive resources.

In addition to building in time for Q&A, the session presenters will invite members of the audience to give examples of dashboards they have created at their Museums and share use-cases of their application. Our presenters will also ask audience members to name big barriers they’ve run into when designing and deploying dashboards, and the tips and tricks they’ve found to overcome them. Lastly, as the strategic plan continues to adapt and evolve, this presentation encourages discussion on how diverse technology tools and resources can support continuous improvement at an institution.
