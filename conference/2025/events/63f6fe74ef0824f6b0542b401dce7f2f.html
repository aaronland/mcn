Creating a Patient-Centered Framework for Art Museum Robot Tours
Tuesday October 21, 2025 1:15pm - 1:35pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
In the face of isolation, illness, and uncertainty, how can museums resonate more deeply with individuals receiving care in hospitals? This case study introduces Arty, The Ringling’s telepresence touring robot, and the Caring Together Framework, a patient-centered approach to digital engagement that brings museum experiences directly to hospital rooms through human-guided robot tours. Built on principles of empathy, interaction, and learning, the framework reimagines digital outreach by prioritizing the emotional and intellectual needs of patients, rather than simply broadcasting content. 

This session will explore how museum staff, healthcare professionals, and technologists collaborated to design personalized, meaningful encounters for patients using telepresence robots. These tours are not just technological interventions—they are crafted social experiences that foster connection, resilience, and inspiration during some of life’s most challenging moments. 

By grounding design decisions in patient voices and emphasizing human interaction over automation, the Caring Together Framework offers a model for how digital museum experiences can be both scalable and deeply personal. Attendees will leave with insights into creating more inclusive, responsive programs that embody care in digital practice.
