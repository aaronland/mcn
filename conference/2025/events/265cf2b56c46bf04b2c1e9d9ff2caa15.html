We are still asking “What is digital?”
Tuesday October 21, 2025 1:15pm - 2:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
In museum technology, you can almost set your watch to someone asking, “What is digital?” Practitioners regularly evaluate digital’s scope of work given how frequently technologies and goals change. We’ve investigated strategies, team models, skills matrices, maturity models—the list goes on! —and have consistently emphasized the value of positioning digital projects and literacy across the entire organization. Once again, the field finds itself in a moment of tectonic shifts, including the impact of generative AI and other rapidly emerging technologies. Our institutions are moving with and against changes in technologies, resources, goals, and politics. How we meet this moment demands both adaptation and reflection. So, let’s once again ask: What is digital?

This session includes panelists from the American Museum of Natural History, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, and the Monterey Bay Aquarium who will explore these guiding questions:

  1. What is now within the scope of “digital”? 
  2. What skills, structures, and resources make digital successful? 
  3. What are the constraints on digital? What are its catalysts?

We’ll cite artifacts from the #MuseTech archive and add contemporary insights/expertise. We’ll also look at other sectors—specifically the government and public interest technologies—for inspiration. After the session, we’ll author a short series of blog posts we’ll write post-conference to offer further context and additional resources.

It’s our hope that by critically examining the evolution, successes, and failures of digital practice, we’ll know where to go next for a more intentional and effective future for digital in museums.
