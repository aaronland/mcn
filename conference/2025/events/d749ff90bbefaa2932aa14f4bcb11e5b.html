Sustainable Futures for Digital Resources: Aligning Mission, Audiences, and Infrastructure
Tuesday October 21, 2025 1:15pm - 2:00pm CDT
Bazinet Lobby - Cinema
You're checked in!
This panel explores how museums and cultural institutions can create digital research resources that balance the needs of both scholarly and broader audiences, as well as internal staff and partners, while supporting long-term institutional resilience. As organizations expand online collections, digital publications, and research platforms, the sustainability of these efforts—both in terms of infrastructure and impact—becomes critical.

Panelists will share case studies and practical decisions that illustrate how digital products can serve as strategic tools for mission alignment, audience development, and institutional adaptability. 

Case studies include:

- Pivoting a print publication to a digital framework, identifying areas where information is, or can be, shared effectively across different uses in the web ecosystem, increasing the efficiency of production and sustainability across projects.

- Reimagining Collections Online to include objects from other collections as a digital catalogue raisonné to support researchers, museum guests, art enthusiasts, and educators by increasing flexibility and facilitating different styles of research information gathering and uses.

- Creating cross-collection and cross-institution integrations that, as a by-product of their original project purpose, increase the efficiency and reduced time required to support new project ideas.

- Migrating a bespoke publishing platform to a shared content management system, ensuring sustainability and workflows for internal stakeholders and creating a more consistent experience.

From rethinking publishing platforms to integrating content more deeply with existing ecosystems, the conversation will address how we understand our users’ needs, collaborate across departments/institutions to harmonize information, and identify where constraints can lead to better user experiences. Digital strategy is evolving with thoughtful design, and responding to changing funding models and staff capacities.
