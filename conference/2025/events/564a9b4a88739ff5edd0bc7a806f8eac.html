The Cost of Curiosity: How to Budget for Interpretive Exhibits Before You Have All the Answers
Tuesday October 21, 2025 2:15pm - 2:35pm CDT
Herzog Building, 5th Floor - Skyline Room
You're checked in!
We've all been there: A Director, Board Member, or partner turns to you and asks, “How much would it cost to do something like this?” It’s just a spark of an idea—there’s no plan, no drawing, and no clear scope. But somehow, you need to provide a thoughtful, ballpark estimate that’s grounded in reality and helps keep momentum moving forward.

This session unpacks how to build a flexible, high-level exhibit budget in the early phases of a project—before you know all the details. We’ll walk through a real-world framework for creating a Rough Order of Magnitude (ROM) budget, drawing on best practices and a dynamic cost calculator that breaks down expenses by type, phase, and scale.

Together, we’ll discuss:

1. Rules of thumb for estimating exhibit costs on a per-square-foot basis 

2. Differentiating capital vs. operating expenses

3. Understanding hard costs (e.g., materials, fabrication, AV hardware) vs. soft costs (e.g., design, planning, media development) 

4. Aligning exhibit phases with architectural design phases to improve predictability 

5. How to use the ROM budget as a decision-making tool, not a crystal ball

Attendees will gain access to a customizable budgeting worksheet that helps build clarity, consensus, and confidence around exhibit planning—even in the early, messy stages. Whether you’re launching a new visitor center or rethinking a gallery on a shoestring, this session will help you respond to the question behind the question: “What’s it going to take to make this real?”
