Reuse, Remix, Resonance: Open Access in the Wild
Wednesday October 22, 2025 2:15pm - 3:00pm CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
Overview

Open access is the gold standard for museums and universities alike, and we know it resonates with public users. But how do we measure this impact, once we release our collections into the wild?

This session will present a diverse range of examples of open access collections reuse, followed by a guided discussion on the types of impact we can and can’t measure (brainstorming how we might creatively capture further future impact.)

Reuse & Resonance Showcase

MCN happens to fall within International Open Access Week this year and the topic continues to hold significance and opportunities across the GLAM sector, as highlighted by over 20 Open GLAM presentations and publications at MCN through the years. We will share updates from previous OA presenters while spotlighting a variety of compelling end-user reuse examples including creative works, research, citizen science, crowdsourcing projects, data visualizations, Wiki knowledgegraphs, NASA hackathons, and third-party cultural aggregators.

Impact & Insights Discussion

In celebration of its five-year open access anniversary, the Smithsonian is conducting a survey and publishing a report covering internal findings and an external landscape survey of the current state of open GLAM collections. The report will be shared out alongside the presentation and we will draw from some resulting conclusions and questions to guide a group discussion on measures of impact as it pertains to audience reuse patterns.

Harvard's Faculty of Arts and Sciences adopted an open access policy in 2008, and will present DASH stories, a clever solution encouraging user qualitative feedback in their open access repository.

We continue to hear from organizations their desire to better understand the motivations and usage patterns of their audiences. Building on examples presented and challenges to measuring reuse, we will guide a discussion on how organizations can creatively capture and encourage sharing of reuse.
