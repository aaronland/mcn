Immersive Storytelling that Goes Beyond Traditional Interpretation
Wednesday October 22, 2025 9:15am - 10:00am CDT
Herzog Building, 3rd Floor - McGuire Theater
You're checked in!
Interpretation from expert voices holds the keys to meaningful and memorable engagement with an object or exhibition. But are curatorial and education voices always the most "expert" of perspectives? Where might we look to traditional models of museum interpretation to influence non-traditional interpretation experiences?

In this session, the presenters will look at models of audio-based interpretation that invite perspectives from beyond an institution's departmental structure.

At the Museum of Arts and Design in New York, a team of producers and actors was enlisted to create an "audio drama" to compliment an exhibition of “worldbuilding” work by artist Saya Woolfalk. Each stop of the audio tour furthered the visitor’s sonic immersion in a fictional narrative expanding on to the exhibition’s utopian themes.. Together, the full tour becomes a comprehensive audio performance that adds an engaging layer of interpretation and invites the visitor to develop a deeper appreciation of the works on view.

MIT List Visual Arts Center invited rapper and MIT professor Lupe Fiasco to create a site-specific rap and field recording project exploring the relationship between rap and MIT's public art collection. Capturing ambient sounds and crafting lyrics in response to each piece, the project transforms public art into a living sonic experience—where rap and visual art intersect in real time.
